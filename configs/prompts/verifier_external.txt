═══════════════════════════════════════════
EVALUATION CRITERIA (priority order)
═══════════════════════════════════════════

0. QUESTION-ANSWER ADEQUACY  ← check FIRST
   Does the Executive Summary directly answer what was asked?
   REJECT if the report answers a different question, hedges
   instead of committing, or fails to provide what was asked.

1. FACTUAL PLAUSIBILITY
   Based on your training knowledge:
   - Do the key claims seem accurate?
   - Are specific numbers in the right ballpark?
   - Are named entities correctly identified and described?
   - Are dates and timelines plausible?

2. INTERNAL CONSISTENCY
   - Do numbers add up? (percentages summing to ~100%, etc.)
   - Do different sections contradict each other?
   - Is the Executive Summary consistent with the body?

3. ANALYTICAL DEPTH
   - Does the report go beyond listing facts?
   - Is there cause-and-effect analysis, not just summaries?
   - Are claims supported by evidence (numbers, dates, names)?

   INSIGHT CHECK (most heavily weighted):
   - Does the report provide original analysis / synthesis?
   - Are there cause-and-effect explanations, trend analysis,
     or connections drawn between findings?
   - Does the report reveal something a reader couldn't get from
     reading any single source?

   REJECT if the report reads like a collection of summaries
   with no original reasoning, comparison, or synthesis.

   SUBSTANCE CHECK:
   - At least 3 distinct analytical sections (not counting
     Executive Summary and Sources)
   - Each body section must be substantive analytical prose

4. COMPLETENESS
   Does every part of the question get addressed?

5. CITATIONS
   Are major factual claims attributed to numbered sources [N]?
   REJECT only if key claims have zero attribution or Sources
   section is empty.

6. LANGUAGE CONSISTENCY
   The entire report must be in the same language as the question.
